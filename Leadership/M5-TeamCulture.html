Module 1: Team Culture Defined

What is a Team?

  • A formal definition of a team is a group of individuals working interdependently for a collective goal.
  • The human dynamics within a team are always present and are important to understand for more effective outcomes.

What is Team Culture?

  • Informally, culture is "the way things are done around here" or "how people behave at work".
  • Culture is a pattern of basic assumptions that a group has developed while coping with problems of external adaptation and internal integration.
  • Culture can be a competitive advantage.

Examples of Team Culture:

  • Relaxed Culture: Team meetings start with informal chat.
  • Rigid/Efficient Culture: Team members are punctual and follow the agenda rigorously.
  • Learning Culture: The team embraces mistakes as opportunities to learn.

Schein's Model (The Iceberg Model):

  • Artifacts and Behaviors: The visible things anyone can observe.
  • Espoused Values: The shared, stated values and norms.
  • Assumptions: The underlying, unquestioned beliefs.

Principles of Effective Teamwork

Three Qualities of Effective Teams:

  • The team performs well. Measured by the value of its output to the recipient.
  • Team members benefit from the experience. Measured by satisfaction, knowledge, and skill development.
  • The team improves its capacity to work together over time. Effective teams get better as they learn and adapt.

To improve over time, teams must be willing to encourage the flow of new information and discuss alternatives, even if it creates temporary "friction."

Leading Teams

The Role of a Leader

  • An effective leader's success is tied to how they manage their team.
  • A leader's role is to create an identity or culture that allows every employee to see themselves within it.
  • Leaders should empower team members to make decisions and solve problems on their own.
  • A leader's job is to hold the "umbrella" for their "superstars" so they can do their best work.

Key Leadership Behaviors and Concepts

  • Modeling Behavior: Leaders serve as models for others; their actions are emulated.
  • Humility: A key element of great leaders, demonstrated through actions that speak louder than words.
  • Valuing People: Leaders spend time getting to know their people and ensuring they feel seen and valued.
  • Learning from Failure: Leaders should create a culture where failure is a learning opportunity, not a source of blame.
