Leadership is a Practice

This experience explores the core idea that leadership is not a fixed trait, but a continuous process of growth and development. Discover the key components that foster this journey and learn how to actively practice becoming a more effective leader.

The Three C's of Leadership Growth

Effective leadership development rests on three interconnected pillars: Curiosity, Courage, and Commitment. Select a concept below to explore its role in your growth and see how they work together in a continuous cycle.

The Practice: Your Leadership Lab

Leadership growth happens through intentional action. Treat your development like a scientific experiment: form a hypothesis, test it with a small action, and reflect on the results. **"I love thinking about ourselves as little scientific labs that are running experiments all the time and that's what's helping us grow."** Use the planner below to design your next leadership experiment.

A Way of Being

Ultimately, leadership is not a set of rules to memorize, but a skill to be honed. It's a way of practicing and being, with the goal of developing yourself to unlock the full power and potential within others. Your growth journey is continuous, built one experiment at a time.
